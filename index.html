Microbial Growth Velocity Curves of Lactobacillus Strains in Fermented Cocoa Beans

This project demonstrates the application of Calculus Derivatives in bioscience by analysing the growth velocity of Lactobacillus bacteria during cocoa bean fermentation in Sabah cocoa estates.

The derivative function f'(x) is used to determine the instantaneous rate of microbial growth over time.

Interactive Growth Velocity Analysis

The graph below shows the microbial population growth over fermentation time. Clicking on any point displays the estimated growth velocity (derivative).

Click on a graph point to view derivative analysis.

Application of Derivatives in Bioscience

In bioscience, derivatives are important for measuring rates of biological changes such as microbial growth, enzyme reactions, and fermentation efficiency.

In this project:

f'(x) = Growth Velocity

A higher derivative value indicates rapid bacterial growth, while lower derivative values indicate slower microbial activity.

This helps cocoa producers in Sabah monitor fermentation quality and improve cocoa bean consistency for industrial production.
